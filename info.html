DISPEL THE MYTHS

While there is a scientific cure for HIV/AIDs/HEP C, anyone living with it can agree that the most difficult part is other people knowing that they have the disease. There are many misconceptions about these diseases, stemming from their discovery back in the 1980s. With our “I am Part of the Cure” campaign, the RHAC hopes to eliminate many of these myths and untruths.

Informational text

EXERCISING

Exercising with a person who has AIDS will not infect you. Even a crisp high five after a great basket or goal will not transmit the disease from person to person.

EATING

Sharing a meal with a friend or family member who has AIDS won’t result in you catching the disease yourself. All it will lead to is improved relationships with that person. Unless you get food all over the place, but that’s on you.

CONTACT

AIDS is not transmitted through any normal form of physical contact such as hugging or kissing.

ADVANCEMENTS

A person who is HIV positive can live for 30+ years due to the advancements of modern medicine. By ingesting HIV proliferation inhibitors, they can live a long, healthy, and happy life.

Living with HIV can be difficult due to the negative bias surrounding the illness. The treatment itself is relatively simple, but due to this stigma, life can be made more difficult due to unwanted distancing from loved ones or being denied a job position due to their medical status.
