About Us

Our mission and vision.

MISSION

The RHAC has set out to eliminate the stigma surrounding people who live with or are at risk from HIV, AIDs, and HEP C.

VISION

Our service users include people living with HIV, AIDS, their partners/family, as well as those concerned.

About the campaign Section

ABOUT THE CAMPAIGN

While stigma refers to an attitude or belief, discrimination is the behaviors that result from those attitudes or beliefs. HIV discrimination is the act of treating people living with HIV differently than those without HIV.

WHAT WE DO

The RHAC works tirelessly to increase access to medical, practical, and psychosocial services for people living with or affected by HIV/AIDS. We also want to integrated programs for these groups that can help provide education and outreach materials to the community.

By establishing partnerships with the community itself, we strengthen our ability to provide these effective services. If you are interested in joining the fight, you can read more about our efforts on our website by clicking the button below!

WHAT WE BELIEVE

Regional HIV/AIDS Connection works from an anti-oppression, anti-racism framework and has adopted several positions statements and definitions that help guide our work with respect to issues such as the criminalization of HIV for non-disclosure, the Vienna Declaration on the criminalization of illicit drug users, health promotion, sex positivity, and diversity. We are currently working on creating position statements on sexism and trans inclusion.
